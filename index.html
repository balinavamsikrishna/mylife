My life summary

0-5years

  • Infants (0-1 year)
  1. Physical: Rolling over, sitting up, crawling, and eventually walking.

  2. Cognitive: Recognizing faces, responding to their name, and exploring objects with their hands and mouth.

  3. Social/Emotional:Smiling, laughing, and showing attachment to caregivers.
  • Toddlers (1-3 years)
  1. Physical: Walking, running, climbing, and beginning to use utensils.

  2. Cognitive: Identifying objects and people, following simple instructions, and starting to use simple sentences.

  3. Social/Emotional: Playing alongside other children, showing independence, and experiencing a range of emotions.
  • Preschoolers (3-5 years)
  1. Physical: Hopping, skipping, and improving hand-eye coordination.

  2. Cognitive: Counting, recognizing letters and numbers, and engaging in imaginative play.

  3. Social/Emotional: Playing cooperatively with peers, understanding rules, and expressing empathy

5 to 22 years

  • Ages 5-10 (Primary School)
  1. Learning Basics: Reading, writing, and basic math skills.

  2. Social Skills: Making friends, sharing, and working in groups.

  3. Physical Activities: Playing sports, running, and developing motor skills.

  4. Creativity: Drawing, painting, and engaging in imaginative play.
  • Ages 11-14 (Middle School)
  1. Advanced Academics: More complex subjects like science, history, and advanced

  2. Activities: Joining clubs, sports teams, and other interest-based groups.

  3. Social Development: Building deeper friendships and learning to navigate social dynamics.

  4. Personal Responsibility: Managing homework, projects, and beginning to understand time management.
  • Ages 15-22 (college/university)
  1. Academic Focus: Preparing for college entrance exams, choosing elective courses, and focusing on career interests.

  2. Leadership Skills: Taking on leadership roles in clubs, sports teams, or student government.

  3. Work Experience: Part-time jobs, internships, or volunteer work to gain real-world Experience

  4. Life Skills: Learning to drive, managing personal finances, and developing independence.
